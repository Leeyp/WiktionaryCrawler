Mandarin[edit]

simpl. 曾经沧海难为水
trad. 曾經滄海難為水

Etymology[edit]

  • c. 385 BCE - 289 BCE: anon, Mencius: Devoting one's energies (part one) (孟子/尽心上)
    孟子曰：孔子登东山而小鲁，登泰山而小天下。故观于海者难为水
    Mencius said, "Having climbed the East Peak (of Mount Tai), Lu seemed small to Confucius. Having climbed to the top of Mount Tai, the whole world below seemed small. Those who have seen the ocean will have difficulty taking rivers seriously. (Wiktionary translation)
  • c. 779 - 831: Yuan Zhen, Missing you (part four of five) (离思五首（其四）)
    曾经沧海难为水，除却巫山不是云。取次花丛懒回顾，半缘修道半缘君。
    Having crossed the vast oceans, I can no longer take a river seriously. If it's not on Mount Wushan, it's not a cloud. I don't care to think back on our leisurely walks among the flowers and shrubs. Half of me is bound to religious devotion, and half is bound to you[1]. (Wiktionary translation)
  1. ^ Legend has it that Yuan Zhen wrote these five poems after his wife passed away

Idiom[edit]

曾经沧海难为水 (simplified, Pinyin céng jīng cānghǎi nán wéi shuǐ, traditional 曾經滄海難為水)

  1. been there, done that
    • 2001: Zhong Jin (仲进) ed., 曾经沧海难为水亚洲首富李兆基徘徊于房地产陷阱 (headline of a news article)
      曾经沧海难为水 亚洲首富李兆基徘徊于房地产陷阱
      Been there, done that ... Asia's richest man, Li Zhaoji, avoids being sucked into the real estate quagmire.
