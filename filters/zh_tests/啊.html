Etymology

Han character

啊 (radical 30 口+8, 11 strokes, cangjie input 口弓中口 (RNLR), four-corner 61020, composition ⿰口阿)

Hanzi

啊 (pinyin ā (a1), á (a2), ǎ (a3), à (a4), a (a5), Wade-Giles a1, a2, a3, a4, a)

Interjection

啊 (traditional and simplified, Pinyin ā)

  1. used by itself to express surprise; ah, oh, ha
    啊！我又赢了！
    Ā! Wǒ yòu yíng le!
    Ha! I've won again!

Particle

啊 (traditional and simplified, Pinyin a)

  1. placed at the end of a sentence to express surprise; softens the request; indicates approval.

Usage notes

Only used after words ending in -ng, ci, zi, si, chi, zhi, shi. See 呀 yā, 哇 wā, 哪 nā, [[]] lā.
