Japanese[edit]

Stroke order

Etymology 1[edit]

Derived in the Heian period from writing the man'yōgana kanji 乃 in the cursive sōsho style.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [no̞]
  • Audio 175px (file)

Syllable[edit]

の (Hepburn romanization no)

  1. The hiragana syllable の (no), whose equivalent in katakana is ノ (no). It is the twenty-fifth syllable of the gojūon order, and its position in gojūon tables is な行お段 (NA-gyō, O-dan; “row NA, section O”).

Etymology 2[edit]

/no2/ *[nə] > /no/ [no].

Particle[edit]

の (romaji no)

  1. (genitive case marker)
    私の意見。
    わたしのいけん。
    watashi no iken.
    My opinion.
  2. (indicates identity or apposition)
    大統領のブッシュ氏。
    だいとうりょうのブッシュし。
    Daitōryō no Busshu-shi.
    Mr Bush, the President.
    山田の馬鹿野郎！
    やまだのばかやろう！
    Yamada no bakayarō!
    Yamada, you stupid jerk!
  3. (a noun modifier)
    数学の分野。
    すうがくのぶんや。
    Sūgaku no bun'ya.
    The field of mathematics.
    緑の車。
    みどりのくるま。
    Midori no kuruma.
    A green car.
  4. (a sentence ending that indicates emphasis or a question, depending on intonation)
    不可能じゃないの？
    ふかのうじゃないの？
    Fukanō janai no?
    It's not possible?
  5. Nominalizes an adjective, verb, or phrase
    食べるのは大好きだ。
    たべるのはだいすきだ。
    Taberu no wa daisuki da.
    I like eating very much.
Usage notes[edit]

Historically written as 乃 or 之.

Kanji reading[edit]

の (romaji no)

  1. 野: field
  2. 述: express, tell

Usage notes[edit]

  • The term is not used in a running Mandarin Chinese text in any region. The symbol is used as a shorthand, or to achieve visual, Japanese-style effect such as on signs, book titles, pamphlet covers or signboards.
